On Dogs and Humanity

Elijah Porter

Published on Oct. 10th

I like dogs. They're pretty neat animals. They like me, I like them. With dogs, I am of the opinion that bigger is better. I also believe that, in general, fluffier is better; however, I understand that fluffiness should be strongly correlated with dog size. It is my opinion that tiny dogs shouldn't be super fluffy and that giant dogs shouldn't have super short fur. This is of course just an opinion, and not one I even feel that strongly about. I just needed something to write about to fulfill my quota.

The opinion I have on dogs that I do feel strongly about is that cats are better. I won't go into depth about why that is true, because, ultimately, I do not know. I recognize and embrace that I am biased and prejudiced towards cats.

Realizing that I still need fifty-seven words, I must mention that tortoises are the best animal. They look funny, act funny, and move slow. What more could be desired from a pet? They have all sorts of different personalities, although I only have experience with aggressive ones. Tortoises that will chase cats and dogs around, while headbutting other tortoises. And that's all my words.
